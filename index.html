About Me

I, Danyelle Barrett, am currently a senior student at the State University of New York at New Paltz. I will be graduating in May 2018 with a Bachelor's of Science in Comupter Science. Ever since I was a young girl, I always had a passion for learning new things, especially in the STEM field. Throughout grade school I stayed at the top of my classes, and graduated from Wallkill Senior High School ranked 8th in my class in 2015. My love for problem solving and mathematics is what lead me to pursue a degree in Computer Science in college. I have come to learn that this field is a perfect match for me. In my three years of undergrad, my love for technology has only continued to grow. From coding in Java to creating web pages, I have truly found my passion. I look forward to creating work that will make an impact on people's lives. Outside of the classroom, I also enjoy hiking, fashion, and staying involved in my community.

These are all programming projects and assignments that I have completed during my undergrad studies at SUNY New Paltz.

  • CPU Scheduling Algorithm: pPH

    A program to implement the preemptive priority high CPU scheduling algorithm. A list of jobs is given in "input.data" and the CPU's schedule as it would be seen in a Gantt chart is outputed to "output.data".

    Tools:

    Java

  • N Queens

    A program that prints out all possible solutions to the N- Queens problem, given an integer, n.

    Tools:

    Java

  • Expression Tree Constructor

    A program that given a file full of mathematical expressions, it will output for each expression its tree, the fully parenthesized infix form, and the solution.

    Tools:

    Java

  • What Do You Meme?

    A web app similar to the card game Bubble Talk. This online game allows users to play against eachother in their own room and compete to have the most wins.

    HTML, CSS, SASS, Javascript, Jquery, Angular4
